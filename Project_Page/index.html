Transforming Ganga Bhogpur into a Model Village of Environmental Awareness

Introduction

Ganga Bhogpur, a beautiful and serene village, has become a symbol of hope for environmental conservation and sustainability. The e-Pahari NGO, working closely with the villagers, focuses on educating people about the importance of nature and implementing strategies for preserving the environment.

From conducting clean-up drives to creating awareness about waste management, e-Pahari’s initiatives have made the village cleaner and greener. Their mission is to connect the community to the environment by highlighting how nature’s preservation directly impacts their livelihood and overall quality of life. Through awareness campaigns, educational workshops, and active involvement in local schools, the NGO is inspiring change at every level.

Features

Focused Environmental Awareness Campaigns

  • 1.   Hosting seminars to educate villagers about issues like deforestation, pollution, and biodiversity loss.
  • 2.   Organizing rallies, posters, and street plays to emphasize the importance of saving nature.

Village-Wide Clean-Up Drives

  • 1.   Regular clean-up programs in public spaces, riversides, and near schools.
  • 2.   Active participation from villagers, students, and volunteers to create cleaner surroundings.

Waste Management Education

  • 1.   Teaching villagers how to segregate waste and reduce plastic use.
  • 2.   Encouraging composting as a way to utilize organic waste.

Educational Efforts for All Ages

  • 1.   Conducting workshops in schools to teach children about conservation.
  • 2.   Special training sessions for adults on adopting eco-friendly practices in daily life.

Community-Driven Programs

  • 1.   Engaging the village in planting trees and preserving green spaces.
  • 2.   Motivating people to act as caretakers of natural resources such as rivers, forests, and soil.

Achievements

Achievements of e-Pahari in Ganga Bhogpur

  • 1.   Over 500 participants in clean-up drives since the start of the program.
  • 2.   A 60% reduction in plastic waste through educational campaigns and alternative solutions.
  • 1.   Introduction of basic waste segregation practices across 80% of households.
